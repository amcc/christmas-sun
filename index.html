Christmas Sun

Christmas time is a celebration of life, just after the sun reaches its lowest point.

A Christmas tree is an evergreen, it is one of the few plants to remain constant all year round and perhaps reminds us that life carries on.

This model shows the position of the sun every month on the 21st day. December 21st is the shortest day and June 21st is the longest day. The angles of the sun are from Edinburgh (or the same latitude elsewhere).

If you're on a mobile phone click on the symbol at the bottom right of the model to view it with Augmented Reality

About this artwork

Each vertical column has a hole which you can look through, or shine a light through to experience the angle of the sun in Edinburgh (or at 56 Â° latitude) during the year. There are a number of small holes at the base of each vertical to represent the month, from 1 - 12.

Ediburgh is used as a basis for the latitude as that is where the artist lives, it's also on a latitude that represents the experience of many in northern parts of Europe, a place with very little sun in winter, but a lot of sun in summer. As humans we experience things in the moment, but viewing in one model the sun over a whole year you can experience something very interesting about our position on the globe.

This artwork is also available as a 3D printed model and a plotted drawing.
